Health Risks Analysis by Demographics


Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

1.)Lack of Healthcare(%) increased as you trended towards lower Household Income(Median). Likewise, the higher the Poverty(%) led to higher Lack of Healthcare(%).

2.)Household Income also seems to have a correlation with the liklihood to smoke. Per our graph you can see that the highest Smoke(%) is seen at the lowest Household Income levels. It is also interesting to note that Utah by far had the lowest smoking percentage, below 10%.

3.)The state of Mississippi had the one of the highest obesity percenatages, along with having the highest poverty percenatge at over 20%. Household Income continues to have a strong role, as you can see in our graph that the highest levels of obesity are seen at the lowest levels of income median.
